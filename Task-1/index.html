History of Kuchipudi

Kuchipudi dates back to the 3rd century BCE, originating in the village of Kuchipudi in Andhra Pradesh. Originally performed by male Brahmin dancers as part of religious theatre, it evolved into a refined solo dance form celebrated for its expressive abhinaya (acting), fast rhythmic footwork, and graceful movements. Today, it is performed by men and women worldwide and continues to be a vibrant part of India’s cultural heritage.
